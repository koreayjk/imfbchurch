Connected Ministry

K-Class
Ministry

Korean college students teaching Americans the Korean language — and opening a door to the mission field.

What is K-Class?

Korean college students at Blinn College and Texas A&M University teach American students the Korean language and culture through the INTO Club — a weekly gathering built on friendship, curiosity, and faith.

Sessions meet 1–2 times per week, with each class running 1 hour following a structured curriculum.

Structured Curriculum — Progressive lessons from Hangul basics to conversational Korean.

Cultural Exchange — Beyond language, students experience Korean culture, food, and traditions.

Faith Community — Run by Korean Christian students, creating natural opportunities for faith conversations.

The Connection

K-Class → Into The World

K-Class students who fall in love with Korean culture are invited to join the Into The World mission trip — where they become English teachers to the very people whose language they've been learning.
